THE CYBER THREAT
How AI Changes the Attack Chain
1
AI accelerates every phase of a cyberattack. Reconnaissance, access, lateral movement, and action all become faster and more adaptive.
2
Defenders must use AI too. The quality of the result depends heavily on the quality of the prompt and how the tool is directed.
3
AI systems create new attack surfaces. Prompt injection, jailbreaking, and related attacks target the AI itself.
THE DATA RISK
The Gap Between AI Usage and AI Governance
1
Traditional controls were not built for AI interactions. Data can move through prompts and responses in ways legacy tools do not fully see.
2
Shadow AI expands the attack surface when employees use unsanctioned AI tools without security review or governance.
3
Hallucination is also a risk. Incorrect output can create operational, legal, and security consequences when it is trusted without verification.
THE DEFENCE STRATEGY
From Awareness to Action
1
The Zscaler AI Security Framework is built around three core pillars: AI Asset Management, Secure Access to AI, and Secure AI Apps and Infrastructure.
2
Governance and Compliance runs across all three. It is a continuous layer, not a separate fourth pillar.
3
Strong defence combines AI speed with human judgment. AI helps detect, investigate, and accelerate response, while humans make the critical decisions.
From this module, you should now be able to:
Five outcomes across the three themes.
1
Describe how attackers use AI to enhance phishing, malware, and other cyber threats.
2
Explain how AI changes the data security risks organisations must manage.
3
Recognise why Shadow AI and legacy controls create visibility and control gaps.
4
Explain the purpose of the Zscaler AI Security Framework in securing AI adoption.
5
Recognise how AI Governance promotes safe usage policies.
